## Fried Liver Attack
**Color:** white
**Tags:** trick, gambit
**PGN:**
```
[Event "Fried Liver Attack"]
[Opening "Fried Liver Attack"]
1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bc4 Nf6 4. Ng5 d5 5. exd5 Nxd5 6. Nxf7 Kxf7 7. Qf3+ Ke6 8. Nc3 Ncb4 9. O-O c6 10. d4 Nxc3 11. bxc3 Na6 12. Re1 Kd7 13. Rxe5
```

**Description:**
**Why it's good:** White sacrifices a knight on f7 to rip open the black king, forcing it to walk into the center where it gets mauled by White's queen and rooks. Even with correct play Black is under severe pressure and must return material.
**Why it's tricky:** Black has no safe king shelter — it must navigate e6, d7, d6 under fire from White's queen and rooks. One misstep is immediately fatal. Most club players have never faced the correct defensive sequence.
**Long-term goals:** Exploit the exposed king with Re1, d4, and Bg5 battery. White typically regains the piece with interest and converts a rook/pawn endgame or mates outright.

**Inaccuracies:**

### Inaccuracy 1: Black plays 7...Kf6 instead of 7...Ke6
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bc4 Nf6 4. Ng5 d5 5. exd5 Nxd5 6. Nxf7 Kxf7 7. Qf3+ Kf6
- **Level:** beginner
- **Why it's bad:** King walks to f6 where it is completely exposed — Qf7# is threatened after Nc3.
- **How to punish:** 8. Nc3 Nce7 9. d4 c6 10. Bg5+ Kf7 11. Ne4 with overwhelming attack; White has multiple mating threats.

### Inaccuracy 2: Black plays 8...Na5 instead of 8...Ncb4
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bc4 Nf6 4. Ng5 d5 5. exd5 Nxd5 6. Nxf7 Kxf7 7. Qf3+ Ke6 8. Nc3 Na5
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** Ignores the threat and allows White to immediately play 9. d4 with tempo, opening the center against the exposed king.
- **How to punish:** 9. d4 Nxc4 10. d5+ Ke7 11. Nd5+ Kd6 12. Bf4 with a devastating attack.

### Inaccuracy 3: Black plays 6...Nb4 instead of 6...Nxd5
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bc4 Nf6 4. Ng5 d5 5. exd5 Nb4
- **Level:** beginner
- **Why it's bad:** Ignores the pawn and gives White free development.
- **How to punish:** 6. Bb3 h6 7. Nf3 e4 8. Ne5 with a solid extra pawn and good position for White.

## Legal's Trap (Légal Trap)
**Color:** white
**Tags:** trick
**PGN:**
```
[Event "Legal's Trap"]
[Opening "Legal's Trap"]
1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bc4 d6 4. Nc3 Bg4 5. h3 Bh5 6. Nxe5 Bxd1 7. Bxf7+ Ke7 8. Nd5#
```

**Description:**
**Why it's good:** White appears to blunder the queen but has calculated a forced checkmate in two. The trap works whenever Black has pinned the f3 knight with Bg4 and has not castled.
**Why it's tricky:** The queen sacrifice looks like a blunder — most players below 1400 will take the queen. The resulting position is a forced mate that cannot be avoided once Black captures on d1.
**Long-term goals:** Pure trick opening — goal is mate on move 8. If Black doesn't fall for it, White has a normal Italian position.

**Inaccuracies:**

### Inaccuracy 1: Black takes the queen with 6...Bxd1
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bc4 d6 4. Nc3 Bg4 5. h3 Bh5 6. Nxe5 Bxd1
- **Level:** beginner
- **Why it's bad:** This is the trap — it leads to forced checkmate.
- **How to punish:** 7. Bxf7+ Ke7 8. Nd5# — the bishop on f7 and knight on d5 combine for checkmate.

### Inaccuracy 2: Black plays 4...Bg4 too early
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bc4 d6 4. Nc3 Bg4
- **Level:** beginner
- **Why it's bad:** The pin is superficially active but walks into the Legal Trap if Black isn't aware.
- **How to punish:** Set up 5. h3 Bh5 6. Nxe5! as above.

### Inaccuracy 3: Black plays 5...Bg6 instead of 5...Bh5
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bc4 d6 4. Nc3 Bg4 5. h3 Bg6
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** Retreating to g6 is too passive; bishop is misplaced and Black loses the pin.
- **How to punish:** 6. d4 exd4 7. Nxd4 Nf6 8. O-O with a strong center and development advantage.

## Danish Gambit
**Color:** white
**Tags:** gambit, trick
**PGN:**
```
[Event "Danish Gambit"]
[Opening "Danish Gambit"]
1. e4 e5 2. d4 exd4 3. c3 dxc3 4. Bc4 cxb2 5. Bxb2 d5 6. Bxd5 Nf6 7. Bxf7+ Kxf7 8. Qxd8 Bb4+ 9. Qd2 Bxd2+ 10. Nxd2
```

**Description:**
**Why it's good:** White sacrifices two pawns to get both bishops aimed at Black's kingside and a massive lead in development. The initiative is ferocious and many games end quickly with a kingside attack.
**Why it's tricky:** Black must return material precisely or get mated. The two bishops raking the long diagonals are very difficult to handle in practical play.
**Long-term goals:** Exploit the development lead with rapid kingside attack. If Black castles early, White presses with e5, Ng5, and Bxf6 to shatter the kingside.

**Inaccuracies:**

### Inaccuracy 1: Black accepts both pawns with 3...dxc3 4...cxb2
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. d4 exd4 3. c3 dxc3 4. Bc4 cxb2 5. Bxb2
- **Level:** beginner
- **Why it's bad:** Black is two pawns up but way behind in development; White's bishops are devastating.
- **How to punish:** 5...d5 6. Bxd5 Nf6 7. Bxf7+! with the classic blow; White regains material with a raging attack.

### Inaccuracy 2: Black plays 3...d3 instead of accepting
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. d4 exd4 3. c3 d3
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** Looks clever but loses time; the pawn on d3 is a target not an asset.
- **How to punish:** 4. Bxd3 Nc6 5. c4 with a strong center and better development for White.

### Inaccuracy 3: Black plays 5...Nf6 instead of 5...d5
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. d4 exd4 3. c3 dxc3 4. Bc4 cxb2 5. Bxb2 Nf6
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** Fails to challenge White's center; White gets to play e5 immediately.
- **How to punish:** 6. e5 Ne4 7. Bxg7 Rg8 8. Bh6 with a massive advantage.

## Halloween Gambit
**Color:** white
**Tags:** gambit, trick
**PGN:**
```
[Event "Halloween Gambit"]
[Opening "Halloween Gambit"]
1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Nc3 Nf6 4. Nxe5 Nxe5 5. d4 Ng6 6. e5 Ng8 7. Bc4 d6 8. O-O dxe5 9. dxe5 Bc5+ 10. Kh1 N8e7 11. Bg5 O-O 12. Nd5
```

**Description:**
**Why it's good:** White sacrifices a whole knight on move 4 to gain a massive pawn center (d4+e4) and driving initiative. Black is forced to spend many moves retreating knights while White storms forward.
**Why it's tricky:** Both of Black's knights get driven back to g6/g8, leaving the kingside open. White's center with e5 and d5 becomes overwhelming. At club level this wins a huge percentage of games.
**Long-term goals:** Maintain the pawn center, activate bishops on c4 and g5, post a knight on d5, and deliver a kingside attack. If Black captures on e5 and d5, White's pieces take over dominant squares.

**Inaccuracies:**

### Inaccuracy 1: Black plays 5...Nc6 instead of 5...Ng6
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Nc3 Nf6 4. Nxe5 Nxe5 5. d4 Nc6
- **Level:** beginner
- **Why it's bad:** Retreating to c6 blocks the c-pawn and is passive; White plays d5 immediately.
- **How to punish:** 6. d5 Ne5 7. f4 Ng6 8. e5 with two powerful central pawns and a raging attack.

### Inaccuracy 2: Black plays 7...Nxe5 instead of 7...d6
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Nc3 Nf6 4. Nxe5 Nxe5 5. d4 Ng6 6. e5 Ng8 7. Bc4 Nxe5
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** Taking the pawn opens the d-file for White's queen and exposes the f7 pawn.
- **How to punish:** 8. dxe5 Bb4+ 9. c3 Bc5 10. Qd5! with a vicious double attack on f7 and the knight.

### Inaccuracy 3: Black castles early without developing
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Nc3 Nf6 4. Nxe5 Nxe5 5. d4 Ng6 6. e5 Ng8 7. Bc4 d6 8. O-O dxe5 9. dxe5 O-O
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** Castling into the attack without solving development; Nd5 and Bg5 are overwhelming.
- **How to punish:** 10. Nd5 Be6 11. Bg5 Bxd5 12. Bxd5 with dominating piece activity.

## Albin Counter-Gambit
**Color:** black
**Tags:** gambit, trick
**PGN:**
```
[Event "Albin Counter-Gambit"]
[Opening "Albin Counter-Gambit"]
1. d4 d5 2. c4 e5 3. dxe5 d4 4. Nf3 Nc6 5. a3 a5 6. g3 Nge7 7. Bg2 Ng6 8. O-O Bc5 9. b3 O-O 10. Bb2 Re8 11. e3 dxe3 12. fxe3 Nxe5
```

**Description:**
**Why it's good:** Black counters d4 aggressively by pushing e5 and sacrificing a pawn to get a strong passed pawn on d4. The d4 pawn is a constant thorn in White's position.
**Why it's tricky:** Black's d4 pawn cramps White and threatens to advance. White must handle the pawn carefully or it becomes dominant. Black gets active piece play in return for the sacrificed material.
**Long-term goals:** Keep the d4 pawn alive as long as possible. Black aims to post pieces actively, castle kingside, and either advance d3 or use the outpost at e5 for a strong knight.

**Inaccuracies:**

### Inaccuracy 1: White plays 3.dxe5 carelessly
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. d4 d5 2. c4 e5 3. dxe5 d4
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** White has accepted the gambit without a plan for handling d4.
- **How to punish:** Black plays Nc6 next, targeting e5, and the d4 pawn is very hard to dislodge.

### Inaccuracy 2: White plays 4.e3 instead of 4.Nf3
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. d4 d5 2. c4 e5 3. dxe5 d4 4. e3
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** Allows 4...Bb4+ and if 5.Nd2 dxe3 6.fxe3 Nf6! with big pressure on e5.
- **How to punish:** 4...Bb4+ 5. Bd2 dxe3 6. fxe3 Qxd2+! 7. Nxd2 Bxd2+ winning a piece.

### Inaccuracy 3: White plays 5.Nbd2 allowing the Lasker Trap
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. d4 d5 2. c4 e5 3. dxe5 d4 4. Nf3 Nc6 5. Nbd2 Bg4 6. h3 Bxf3 7. Nxf3 Bb4+
- **Level:** advanced
- **Why it's bad:** White falls into a forced sequence where Black wins material.
- **How to punish:** 8. Nd2 Nge7 9. a3 Ba5 with active play; Black maintains compensation.

## Vienna Gambit
**Color:** white
**Tags:** gambit
**PGN:**
```
[Event "Vienna Gambit"]
[Opening "Vienna Gambit"]
1. e4 e5 2. Nc3 Nf6 3. f4 d5 4. fxe5 Nxe4 5. Nf3 Bb4 6. Qe2 Nxc3 7. dxc3 Bxc3+ 8. Bd2 Bxd2+ 9. Qxd2 O-O 10. Qe3 Nc6 11. Bd3 Re8 12. O-O
```

**Description:**
**Why it's good:** White plays f4 early to support a pawn center or launch a kingside attack. The Vienna Gambit combines the Vienna Game's solidity with f4's aggression, creating unbalanced positions where White has good winning chances.
**Why it's tricky:** Black cannot easily equalize — if they take on f4, White gets a strong center; if they don't, White gets a massive f4-e4 pawn duo. The position is full of tactical tension.
**Long-term goals:** After the gambit, White aims to post pieces actively, keep the e5 pawn or use it as an outpost, and create a kingside attack via Bd3, Qe3, and eventually a rook to the f-file.

**Inaccuracies:**

### Inaccuracy 1: Black plays 3...exf4 instead of 3...d5
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nc3 Nf6 3. f4 exf4
- **Level:** beginner
- **Why it's bad:** Accepting the gambit gives White a powerful center with e4-d4.
- **How to punish:** 4. e5 Ng8 5. Nf3 d6 6. d4 dxe5 7. dxe5 with a large center and bishop pair.

### Inaccuracy 2: Black plays 4...Nxe5 instead of 4...Nxe4
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nc3 Nf6 3. f4 d5 4. fxe5 Nxe5
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** The knight on e5 is immediately attacked; Black loses time retreating.
- **How to punish:** 5. d4 Nc6 6. Nf3 Bg4 7. Be2 with a solid center and comfortable development.

### Inaccuracy 3: Black plays 5...Be7 instead of 5...Bb4
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nc3 Nf6 3. f4 d5 4. fxe5 Nxe4 5. Nf3 Be7
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** Passive; allows White to consolidate the e5 pawn and develop smoothly.
- **How to punish:** 6. d4 O-O 7. Bd3 Nxc3 8. bxc3 c5 9. O-O with a strong center and bishop pair advantage.

## Scotch Game
**Color:** white
**Tags:** standard
**PGN:**
```
[Event "Scotch Game"]
[Opening "Scotch Game"]
1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. d4 exd4 4. Nxd4 Nf6 5. Nxc6 bxc6 6. e5 Qe7 7. Qe2 Nd5 8. c4 Nb6 9. Nd2 Qe6 10. b3 a5 11. Bb2 a4 12. O-O-O axb3 13. axb3
```

**Description:**
**Why it's good:** The Scotch was revived by Kasparov in the 1990s and remains a top-level weapon. White opens the center immediately on move 3, avoiding the Spanish torture and getting early active play.
**Why it's tricky:** After 4.Nxd4 and 5.Nxc6, Black must navigate the asymmetric pawn structure. White often castles queenside and attacks, while Black tries to use the bishop pair and active pieces.
**Long-term goals:** White aims for piece activity and kingside attack after O-O-O. The pawn structure with c4 gives White queenside space. White must prevent Black's bishop pair from dominating.

**Inaccuracies:**

### Inaccuracy 1: Black plays 4...Bc5 instead of 4...Nf6
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. d4 exd4 4. Nxd4 Bc5
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** The bishop is exposed and White can play 5.Nb3 forcing it to move again.
- **How to punish:** 5. Nb3 Bb6 6. Nc3 Nf6 7. Bg5 with active development and pressure.

### Inaccuracy 2: Black plays 6...Nd5 instead of 6...Qe7
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. d4 exd4 4. Nxd4 Nf6 5. Nxc6 bxc6 6. e5 Nd5
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** The knight on d5 is eventually dislodged; 6...Qe7 is the main move to fight e5.
- **How to punish:** 7. Nd2 Bc5 8. Nb3 Bb6 9. Qg4! threatening Qxg7 and g2-g3-g4 attack.

### Inaccuracy 3: Black plays 5...dxc6 instead of 5...bxc6
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. d4 exd4 4. Nxd4 Nf6 5. Nxc6 dxc6
- **Level:** beginner
- **Why it's bad:** Closes the d-file but weakens the queenside pawn structure more severely.
- **How to punish:** 6. Qxd8+ Kxd8 7. Bc4 Be6 8. Bxe6 fxe6 9. f3 with a better endgame for White.

## Göring Gambit
**Color:** white
**Tags:** gambit, trick
**PGN:**
```
[Event "Göring Gambit"]
[Opening "Göring Gambit"]
1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. d4 exd4 4. c3 dxc3 5. Bc4 cxb2 6. Bxb2 d6 7. O-O Nf6 8. Nc3 Be7 9. Nd5 Nxd5 10. exd5 Ne5 11. Nxe5 dxe5 12. Qh5
```

**Description:**
**Why it's good:** Similar to the Danish Gambit but arising from the Scotch structure. White sacrifices one or two pawns to get both bishops aimed at Black's kingside with a big development lead.
**Why it's tricky:** The tempo gained from the c3 pawn sacrifice means White's development is ahead of normal gambit lines. Black must be precise — one wrong move leads to a mating attack.
**Long-term goals:** Get both bishops on long diagonals, castle, and attack on the kingside. The Nd5 outpost and Bb2 diagonal are key weapons.

**Inaccuracies:**

### Inaccuracy 1: Black accepts the double gambit
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. d4 exd4 4. c3 dxc3 5. Bc4 cxb2 6. Bxb2
- **Level:** beginner
- **Why it's bad:** White has massive compensation; two bishops plus castle rights vs. two pawns.
- **How to punish:** 6...d6 7. O-O Nf6 8. Nd5! with a powerful centralized knight and attack.

### Inaccuracy 2: Black plays 4...d5 instead of 4...dxc3
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. d4 exd4 4. c3 d5
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** This actually leads to sharp play but White gets good counterplay.
- **How to punish:** 5. exd5 Qxd5 6. cxd4 Bg4 7. Be2 Bb4+ 8. Nc3 with solid development and pressure.

### Inaccuracy 3: Black plays 7...Be7 too passively
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. d4 exd4 4. c3 dxc3 5. Bc4 cxb2 6. Bxb2 d6 7. O-O Be7
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** Too slow; White immediately launches Nd5 before Black completes development.
- **How to punish:** 8. Nd5 Nf6 9. Nxf6+ Bxf6 10. Ng5 with an immediate attack on f7.

## Four Knights Game: Spanish Variation
**Color:** white
**Tags:** standard
**PGN:**
```
[Event "Four Knights Game Spanish Variation"]
[Opening "Four Knights Game Spanish Variation"]
1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Nc3 Nf6 4. Bb5 Bb4 5. O-O O-O 6. d3 d6 7. Bg5 Ne7 8. Nd5 Nxd5 9. exd5 c6 10. dxc6 bxc6 11. Bxc6 Bd7 12. Bxd7 Qxd7 13. Re1
```

**Description:**
**Why it's good:** A solid, symmetrical opening that leads to rich middlegames. Both sides develop naturally and the game is decided in the middlegame rather than the opening. Popular at high levels due to its solidity.
**Why it's tricky:** The symmetry is deceptive — small inaccuracies create large imbalances. White's Bg5 pin and Nd5 maneuver are particularly dangerous for unprepared opponents.
**Long-term goals:** After the exchanges, White aims to dominate the open files with rooks, use the bishop pair in an open position, and target Black's isolated or doubled pawns.

**Inaccuracies:**

### Inaccuracy 1: Black plays 4...Nd4 (Rubinstein Variation)
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Nc3 Nf6 4. Bb5 Nd4
- **Level:** advanced
- **Why it's bad:** Aggressive but not fully equalizing; White can get a solid advantage with correct play.
- **How to punish:** 5. Nxd4 exd4 6. e5 dxc3 7. exf6 Qxf6 8. dxc3 with good center and bishop pair.

### Inaccuracy 2: Black plays 5...d6 instead of 5...O-O
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Nc3 Nf6 4. Bb5 Bb4 5. O-O d6
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** Slow; White plays d4 to challenge the center while Black's king is still in the center.
- **How to punish:** 6. d4 exd4 7. Nxd4 Bd7 8. Nf5 g6 9. Nh6 with a strong position.

### Inaccuracy 3: Black plays 7...Bxc3 instead of 7...Ne7
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Nc3 Nf6 4. Bb5 Bb4 5. O-O O-O 6. d3 d6 7. Bg5 Bxc3
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** Giving up the bishop pair without reason; White recaptures with bxc3 getting strong center control.
- **How to punish:** 8. bxc3 h6 9. Bh4 g5 10. Bg3 Nh5 11. Nd2 with queenside expansion and strong center.

## Petroff Defense
**Color:** black
**Tags:** standard
**PGN:**
```
[Event "Petroff Defense"]
[Opening "Petroff Defense"]
1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nf6 3. Nxe5 d6 4. Nf3 Nxe4 5. d4 d5 6. Bd3 Bd6 7. O-O O-O 8. c4 c6 9. Re1 Bf5 10. Bxe4 Bxe4 11. Nc3 Bxf3 12. Qxf3 dxc4 13. Qxc6 Nc6
```

**Description:**
**Why it's good:** The Petroff is one of the most solid defenses to 1.e4. By mirroring White's knight development, Black equalizes immediately. Preferred by players who want to draw with Black or play for small advantages in the endgame.
**Why it's tricky:** White must be careful — taking the e5 pawn without 3.Nxe5 leads to trouble, and the resulting positions after d4 are theoretically demanding. Many White players are under-prepared.
**Long-term goals:** Black aims for equality and then exploits any White inaccuracy in the endgame. The symmetric pawn structure often leads to draws, but Black can play for a win if White overreaches.

**Inaccuracies:**

### Inaccuracy 1: White plays 3.d4 instead of 3.Nxe5
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nf6 3. d4
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** Allows 3...exd4 and Black can equalize comfortably with 4.e5 Ne4 5.Qxd4 d5.
- **How to punish:** 3...exd4 4. e5 Ne4 5. Qxd4 d5 6. exd6 Nxd6 with good piece activity for Black.

### Inaccuracy 2: White plays 3.Nxe5 Nxe4 without 4.Qe2
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nf6 3. Nxe5 Nxe4
- **Level:** beginner
- **Why it's bad:** If White doesn't play the correct 3...d6 4.Nf3 sequence, Black gets counterplay.
- **How to punish:** After 3...Nxe4 Black regains the pawn with piece activity if White isn't careful.

### Inaccuracy 3: White plays 6.Bd3 Nc6 without targeting d5
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nf6 3. Nxe5 d6 4. Nf3 Nxe4 5. d4 d5 6. Bd3 Nc6
- **Level:** advanced
- **Why it's bad:** White must challenge d5 with c4; delay gives Black a comfortable position.
- **How to punish:** Black plays Bd6 and O-O with a perfectly fine position; White's center becomes a target.

## Nimzo-Indian Defense
**Color:** black
**Tags:** standard
**PGN:**
```
[Event "Nimzo-Indian Defense"]
[Opening "Nimzo-Indian Defense"]
1. d4 Nf6 2. c4 e6 3. Nc3 Bb4 4. e3 O-O 5. Bd3 d5 6. Nf3 c5 7. O-O Nc6 8. a3 Bxc3 9. bxc3 dxc4 10. Bxc4 Qc7 11. Bd3 e5 12. Qc2 Re8 13. e4 exd4 14. cxd4 c4 15. Bc2
```

**Description:**
**Why it's good:** One of the best defenses to d4. Black immediately places pressure on White's c3 knight and doubles White's pawns if the knight moves. The bishop on b4 creates long-term structural damage.
**Why it's tricky:** After Bxc3, Black gives up the bishop pair but saddles White with doubled c-pawns. Black gets dynamic compensation through piece activity and pressure on the weakened queenside.
**Long-term goals:** After Bxc3 bxc3, Black targets the c4 pawn and uses piece activity to neutralize White's bishop pair. Black aims for an active knight outpost on e4 or c4 and queenside pressure.

**Inaccuracies:**

### Inaccuracy 1: White plays 4.Qc2 instead of 4.e3
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. d4 Nf6 2. c4 e6 3. Nc3 Bb4 4. Qc2
- **Level:** advanced
- **Why it's bad:** The Qc2 system is playable but Black has 4...O-O and d5/c5 with comfortable play.
- **How to punish:** 4...O-O 5. a3 Bxc3+ 6. Qxc3 b6 with a solid hedgehog setup.

### Inaccuracy 2: White plays 5.Ne2 instead of 5.Bd3
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. d4 Nf6 2. c4 e6 3. Nc3 Bb4 4. e3 O-O 5. Ne2
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** Ne2 blocks the bishop and is passive; Black can immediately play d5 with equality.
- **How to punish:** 5...d5 6. a3 Bd6 7. cxd5 exd5 8. g3 Re8 with good central control.

### Inaccuracy 3: White plays 6.cxd5 too early
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. d4 Nf6 2. c4 e6 3. Nc3 Bb4 4. e3 O-O 5. Bd3 d5 6. cxd5
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** Releases the tension prematurely; Black recaptures with exd5 getting a strong center.
- **How to punish:** 6...exd5 7. Nge2 Re8 8. O-O Bd6 with active piece play and central control.

## King's Indian Defense
**Color:** black
**Tags:** standard
**PGN:**
```
[Event "King's Indian Defense Classical"]
[Opening "King's Indian Defense Classical"]
1. d4 Nf6 2. c4 g6 3. Nc3 Bg7 4. e4 d6 5. Nf3 O-O 6. Be2 e5 7. O-O Nc6 8. d5 Ne7 9. Ne1 Nd7 10. Nd3 f5 11. Bd2 Nf6 12. f3 f4 13. c5 g5 14. Rc1 Ng6
```

**Description:**
**Why it's good:** The Veresov Attack (1.d4 Nf6 2.Nc3 d5 3.Bg5) is a solid system that avoids main-line theory. White develops quickly and pins the knight immediately. A good surprise weapon that leads to rich middlegames.
**Why it's tricky:** Black can't use standard d4 opening theory; the Bg5 pin complicates normal development. White aims for a quick kingside attack or queenside expansion depending on Black's response.
**Long-term goals:** White aims for e4 at some point to open the center, uses the bishop pair, and seeks to create imbalances. The b4-b5 queenside advance is a common plan to restrict Black.

**Inaccuracies:**

### Inaccuracy 1: Black plays 3...c5 against the Veresov
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. d4 Nf6 2. Nc3 d5 3. Bg5 c5
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** Counter in the center is right idea but White plays dxc5 and develops actively.
- **How to punish:** 4. Bxf6 exf6 5. dxc5 Bxc5 6. e3 O-O 7. Bd3 with a solid position and the bishop pair.

### Inaccuracy 2: Black plays 3...h6 to chase the bishop
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. d4 Nf6 2. Nc3 d5 3. Bg5 h6 4. Bxf6 exf6
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** Giving up the knight pair; the doubled f-pawns reduce Black's flexibility.
- **How to punish:** 5. e3 c6 6. Bd3 Bd6 7. Nf3 Nd7 8. O-O with a solid edge for White.

### Inaccuracy 3: White plays 4.f3 instead of 4.Nf3
- **Moves to bad move:** 1. d4 Nf6 2. Nc3 d5 3. Bg5 Nbd7 4. f3
- **Level:** intermediate
- **Why it's bad:** Weakens e3 and g3; Black plays c5 and challenges the center.
- **How to punish:** 4...c5 5. dxc5 e6 6. e4 dxe4 7. fxe4 Bxc5 with active piece play.
